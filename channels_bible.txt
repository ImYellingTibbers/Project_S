CLIPPIE BIBLE — 4 CHANNEL NETWORK (v1)
=====================================

NOTE / CORRECTION
- Channel 1 = Confessional Horror (POV / “This happened to me”)
- Channel 3 = Real-World Mistakes & Saves (Composite Realism)
- Channel 4 = Impossible Scenarios (Science-ish Entertainment)
- Channel 2 = Choose-Your-Own-Adventure Dilemmas (Two Doors)

------------------------------------------------------------
GLOBAL RULES (APPLY TO ALL CHANNELS)
------------------------------------------------------------

1) Each channel must look and feel distinct
- Different template preference
- Different VO tone
- Different visual style
- Different pacing and opening phrase

2) Keep Shorts pacing tight
- Hook within 0.0–1.0s
- New beat / new visual change every 2–3 seconds
- Remove any slow “setup paragraph” energy

3) Captions must be phone-readable
- Large text, high contrast + stroke/outline
- Place away from UI and keep consistent per channel
- Karaoke/word-highlight ON if available

4) Human QC pass before upload (15–45 seconds)
- Hook hits instantly
- Captions readable
- Visuals not off-topic / uncanny / distracting
- Pacing has no dead space

------------------------------------------------------------
CHANNEL 1 — CONFESSIONAL HORROR (POV / “THIS HAPPENED TO ME”)
------------------------------------------------------------

Goal
- High retention via dread → escalation → final sting.

Clippie Template
- Primary: AI Story Video
- Backup: Text Stories (if AI Story feels too slow)

Visual Style
- Dark Comic (or “Cinematic Horror” if available)

VO Tone (you choose exact voice later)
- Low, controlled, intimate
- Slight urgency as it escalates
- Confessional delivery (NOT announcer)

Target Length
- 35–55 seconds

Caption Settings (recommended)
- Large
- Center-lower placement (avoid UI overlap)
- High contrast + stroke
- Karaoke/word-highlight ON if available
- Serious look (no emoji clutter)

Pacing Rules (non-negotiable)
- Hook in 0.0–1.0s
- New beat every 2–3 seconds
- No slow time/location openings unless line 1 slaps

Structure Template
1) Hook (1 line)
2) Grounded detail (rule/object/sound)
3) Escalation x3
4) Proof moment (“I recorded it” / “I checked the camera”)
5) Final sting (last 1–2 seconds)

Extra Standardization
- Subtle low rumble / tension bed if music is used
- No happy transitions
- Prefer hard cuts over “cute” motion presets

------------------------------------------------------------
CHANNEL 2 — CHOOSE-YOUR-OWN-ADVENTURE DILEMMAS (TWO DOORS)
------------------------------------------------------------

Goal
- Retention + comments via “second door reveal” curiosity.

Clippie Template
- Primary: Would You Rather
- Backup: Text Stories

Visual Style
- Dark Comic (punchy + recognizable)

VO Tone (you choose exact voice later)
- Fast, clear, slightly intense
- “Game master” energy (NOT goofy)
- Builds pressure

Target Length
- 25–40 seconds

Caption Settings (recommended)
- Largest readable
- Top or mid placement (keep options readable)
- Strong outline/stroke
- Emphasize: “DOOR A / DOOR B”, “10 SECONDS”, “CHOOSE”

Pacing Rules
- Present choice by ~7 seconds
- Reveal Door A outcome first (worse outcome)
- Reveal Door B outcome second (correct/survival)
- End with: “What would you pick?”

Structure Template
1) Hook: “You have 10 seconds.”
2) Scenario (one sentence)
3) Door A + Door B (clear labels)
4) Door A outcome (short + brutal)
5) Door B outcome (short + twist)
6) CTA: “What would you pick?”

Extra Standardization
- On-screen timer visual if possible
- Repeat phrasing: “Door A…” then “Door B…”

------------------------------------------------------------
CHANNEL 3 — REAL-WORLD MISTAKES & SAVES (COMPOSITE REALISM)
------------------------------------------------------------

Goal
- High-value, shareable “this could happen to you” stories.

Clippie Template
- Primary: AI Voiceover Video
- Backup: Text Stories

Visual Style
- Polished Cartoon (clean + brand-safe)

VO Tone (you choose exact voice later)
- Confident, practical, slightly urgent
- “Older brother warning you”
- NOT dramatic horror voice

Target Length
- 20–35 seconds

Caption Settings (recommended)
- Medium-large
- Lower third
- High contrast stroke
- Emphasize numbers: $, dates, “1 rule”, “DO THIS”

Pacing Rules
- Hook is always cost/risk-based
- Keep it simple: mistake → consequence → fix

Structure Template
1) Hook: “This mistake costs people $___ in ___.”
2) The mistake (1 line)
3) What happens next (1–2 lines)
4) The fix (1 line)
5) Micro CTA: “Save this.”

Extra Standardization
- Optional labels: “MISTAKE / WHAT HAPPENS / FIX”
- Neutral music or none

------------------------------------------------------------
CHANNEL 4 — IMPOSSIBLE SCENARIOS (SCIENCE-ISH ENTERTAINMENT)
------------------------------------------------------------

Goal
- Curiosity + escalation + “holy shit” ending (entertainment-first).

Clippie Template
- Primary: AI Voiceover Video
- Backup: Top 5 Video (countdown consequences)

Visual Style
- Cinematic (or Ultra Realism if it stays clean)

VO Tone (you choose exact voice later)
- Authoritative + excited
- Documentary narrator, faster pace

Target Length
- 35–55 seconds

Caption Settings (recommended)
- Medium-large
- Upper-middle placement (leave room for visuals)
- Stroke ON
- Highlight numbers: “8 minutes”, “300 mph”, “2 seconds”

Pacing Rules
- Never start with “Have you ever wondered…”
- Start with a claim: “You’d die before you saw it.”
- Escalate 3 times minimum

Structure Template
1) Premise hook (1 line)
2) Immediate consequence
3) Bigger consequence
4) Catastrophic or weird consequence
5) Final line: “And that’s the best-case scenario.”

Extra Standardization
- Sound hits on escalation beats (if available)
- Avoid comedy SFX; keep awe + dread

------------------------------------------------------------
CHANNEL 5 — URBAN LEGEND HORROR STORIES
------------------------------------------------------------

Goal
- “Local legend” horror told like a micro-documentary: specific place + rule + consequence + proof.
- High retention via mystery → rule reveal → escalating cases → present-day sting.

Clippie Template
- Primary: Text Stories
- Backup: AI Story Video (if you want more scene variety / faster cuts)

Visual Style
- Cinematic (preferred) OR Dark Comic (if Cinematic looks uncanny)
- “Archival” vibe: maps, warning signs, faded photos, newspaper-style frames (when possible)

VO Tone (you choose exact voice later)
- Calm documentary narrator with controlled dread
- Confident, factual delivery (NOT whispery, NOT overacted)
- Slight intensity increases near the proof + ending sting

Target Length
- 40–60 seconds

Caption Settings (recommended)
- Medium-large
- Center-lower placement (safe zone)
- High contrast + stroke
- Karaoke/word-highlight ON if available
- Emphasize key words: PLACE, TIME, RULE, WARNING, PROOF

Pacing Rules (non-negotiable)
- Hook in 0.0–1.5s
- The “RULE” must appear by 6–10s
- New beat every 2–3 seconds (no dead air)
- Proof moment by 25–40s (poster / report / tape / dashcam)
- End with a present-day sting or warning (last 2–3s)

Structure Template
1) Hook (1 line): “In [town], there’s a [road/room/bridge] nobody uses after midnight.”
2) Grounded detail: exact time + landmark + local phrase (1–2 lines)
3) The Rule (single sentence): “If you hear/see ___, don’t ___.”
4) First case (quick): “First person who broke it…”
5) Escalation case (worse): “Second one…”
6) Proof moment: “They found a tape / missing poster / report / voicemail…”
7) Present-day sting: “And the sign is still there… freshly repainted.”

Extra Standardization
- Every story must include:
  - 1 named place element (real-sounding even if fictional)
  - 1 exact time stamp (2:17, 3:11, 11:42)
  - 1 “RULE” sentence stated clearly
  - 1 evidence/proof insert (missing poster, dashcam frame, police report style, voicemail waveform)
  - 1 warning or sting ending (“If you ever hear it… don’t stop walking.”)
  - Keep transitions simple + tense (avoid playful motion presets)
  - If music is used: low, subtle tension bed (no upbeat tracks)
